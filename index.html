Customer Analytics

Customer Churn Insights Dashboard

Understanding why customers leave and how to keep them.

Problem: A subscription-based product needed to identify which customers were most at risk of churn and why.

What I did: Cleaned and joined usage, billing, and support data in SQL and Python, built a simple churn model, and designed an interactive Power BI dashboard with filters by segment, region, and plan type.

Outcome: Highlighted three high-risk segments and key churn drivers, helping prioritize retention actions and focus retention campaigns where they matter most.

SQL Python (Pandas) Power BI Data Cleaning Churn Modeling

Sales Performance Analysis

From raw transaction data to clear revenue insights.

Problem: Large volumes of sales data but no unified view of product, channel, or regional performance.

What I did: Aggregated order and customer data into a clean model, created KPIs (revenue, average order value, repeat rate), and built time-series views to compare performance across months, products, and marketing channels.

Outcome: Revealed top-performing products and identified a low-performing marketing channel, guiding budget reallocation and more focused merchandising.

Personal ¬∑ Gaming

Gaming Engagement Analytics

Exploring how play patterns relate to player retention.

Problem: Understand how early-session behavior relates to long-term engagement in a gaming dataset.

What I did: Analyzed session length, frequency, and day-1/7/30 retention; segmented players based on play style and visualized retention curves and progression patterns.

Outcome: Found that early consistent play was strongly associated with long-term retention and identified patterns to inform onboarding, rewards, and in-game events.
